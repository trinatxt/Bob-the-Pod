The Problem

Why it
matters

Hybrid work is permanent, but Singapore homes are shrinking. Existing office pods occupy fixed floor space, can't adapt to residential use, and were never designed for home environments.


BOB the Pod addresses this directly: a collapsible, IoT-enabled workspace that folds flat when not in use and deploys into a fully equipped smart office in seconds.

01
React Native Mobile App

Built two editions of the app (Home and Office) from the ground up in React Native. The app gives users real-time control over lighting colour and brightness, fan speed, and environmental presets — all from their phone. I designed the UI architecture, wrote the API integration layer, and built the pod booking and scheduling system for the office edition.

02
AWS Backend & Database

Designed and maintained the database schemas, wrote SQL queries, and built the backend logic that keeps user sessions, pod states, and environmental settings in sync across devices. All commands route through the AWS-hosted backend before reaching the pod hardware.

AWS PostgreSQL Schema design Session management

ESP32 IoT & Hardware Integration

Wired and soldered the LED lighting and ventilation fan circuits, then integrated the ESP32 microcontroller with the AWS backend over MQTT pub/sub. Every tap in the app triggers a live response in the physical pod — lighting changes, fan speeds up, the pod reacts in real time.

Process

Three rounds
of iteration

01
Origami + accordion folding structure

Explored collapsible pod structures inspired by origami and accordion mechanisms. Built small-scale prototypes from laser-cut plywood and 3D-printed joints to test deployment. A manual folding chair on wall rails was also explored.

❌ Too many moving parts — instability, misalignment, inconvenient to operate
02
Aluminium framing + pulley-based deployment

Switched to aluminium frames for structural rigidity and introduced automated chair deployment via a pulley system. Also began integrating smart environmental systems — adjustable lighting, ventilation, and IoT infrastructure planning.

❌ High fabrication complexity, bulky counterweights, exposed tension cables
03
Slotted plywood + telescopic PVC + linear actuators

The final design used slotted plywood panels, telescopic PVC rails, retractable acoustic curtains (triple-layer wool flannel), and electrically powered linear actuators for chair and table height adjustment. Custom 3D-printed connectors secured the rail system. All IoT, app, and hardware systems were fully integrated and demonstrated live.

✓ Stable, practical, fully functional — demonstrated at SUTD capstone exhibition
